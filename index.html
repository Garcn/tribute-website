Mac Miller, the Prodigy, Talented and Consecrated.

Mac Miller started rapping at age 14, before changing his name to Mac Miller, he was known as EZ Mac and released a mixtape, My Mackin, Mac Miller was part of the group The Ill Spoken along with rapper Beedie.

In 2009, at age 17, he made the final four in Rhyme Calisthenics, the MC's rhyming competition at the Shadow Lounge. As early as 2010, Mac Miller was signed with Rostrum Records, Benjy Grinberg, President of the Tribune Rate Miller, while recording with Wiz Khalifa at ID Labs. Mac Miller started to show on his first tour, but chose Rostrum because of the location and attention, KIDS went with the label in August 10010, thus going out on his first tour, to be launched Incredably Dope Tour, as told of the locality and to be where Wiz Khads was recognized in Pittsburgh's Hip Hop Year Award.

Also in 2010, “Best Day Ever” was released, with the single “Donald Trump” and the hits “Wear My Hat” and “All Around the World”, November 22, 2010, his first single, “Knock Knock”, debuted on YouTube, on March 29, 2011, he released the EP On and On Beyond. The six-track work was produced by Rostrum Records. On May 11, 2014, Miller independently released his tenth solo mixtape, Faces.

The Divine Feminine, was released on September 16, 2016, featuring the single, "Dang!", featuring vocals by Anderson Paak.

After 2 years and a few months of singles, Mac Miller released "Small Worlds", the first single from the album, Swimming, released on August 3, his latest work was "Self care", which passed 50 million.

Mac had a 3-year relationship with singer Ariana Grande, in which it was a little troubled, but very popular. And their latest release was the posthumous album Circles, in 2019, on which Mac and some producers were already finalizing for a brief release.

In September 2018, after years of struggling with drugs and depression, Mac passed away, leaving all his love for music, all his contributions made, and all his talent demonstrated.

He is still greatly missed, but he still lives on for eternity through his legacy.
